Samsung Refrigerator Not Cooling? Here's What to Check First

6 min read By RapidFix Kenya Technical Team July 4, 2026

A warm Samsung refrigerator is more than just an inconvenience – it means spoiled food and potential health risks. In Nairobi's humid climate, fridge efficiency drops even further, making cooling problems more critical.

Before calling for expensive repairs, work through this systematic troubleshooting guide to identify the issue and potentially save costs.

Immediate Checks When Your Samsung Fridge Stops Cooling

  1. Temperature setting verification – Ensure fridge is set to 3-5°C and freezer to -18°C
  2. Power indicator status – Check if the display is on and cooling light is active
  3. Door seal inspection – Close a piece of paper in the door; if it pulls out easily, the seal is faulty
  4. Condenser coil location – Pull the fridge away from the wall and check for dust buildup

Common Causes of Samsung Fridge Cooling Failure

1. Dirty Condenser Coils (Most Common)

In Nairobi's dusty environment, condenser coils accumulate dust within weeks. This forces the compressor to work harder, reducing cooling efficiency by up to 30%.

2. Faulty Thermostat or Temperature Sensor

Samsung's digital thermostats can fail due to power surges. Symptoms include:

  • Fridge running continuously without reaching temperature
  • Display showing random temperatures
  • Clicking sounds from the control panel

3. Blocked Air Vents Inside

Overpacking your fridge blocks internal airflow. Check that items aren't touching the rear wall where cold air vents are located.

People Also Ask: Samsung Fridge Issues

Why does my Samsung fridge stop cooling after a power cut?

Power cuts can cause the compressor to lock up. After power returns, leave the fridge unplugged for 5 minutes before restarting. Persistent issues indicate refrigerant pressure problems.

How often should I clean my Samsung fridge coils in Nairobi?

Due to heavy dust in Nairobi, clean coils every 2-4 weeks. In coastal regions like Mombasa, weekly cleaning may be necessary.

My Samsung fridge is making clicking sounds – what does it mean?

Clicking often indicates a failing start relay or compressor issue. If the sound persists more than 10 seconds, stop the fridge and call a technician.

Kenya-Specific Considerations

Kenya's voltage fluctuations (often 220V-250V instead of standard 230V) stress Samsung's inverter compressors. Consider:

  • Installing a voltage stabilizer for units above 200L capacity
  • Regularly cleaning coils due to Nairobi's Harmattan season dust
  • Ensuring proper ventilation space (10cm minimum) around fridge sides

When to Call for Professional Repair

Contact RapidFix if you've checked all above and the fridge still isn't cooling:

  • Compressor running but no cooling for 4+ hours
  • Unusual noises (grinding, hissing, continuous clicking)
  • Water leaking from the bottom
  • Temperature display showing errors (FF, rd, etc.)

Pro Tip: Keep a thermometer in your fridge. Normal operating temperature is 3-5°C. Above 8°C means spoilage risk.
