Frequently Asked Questions

Why is my fridge running but not cooling?

This could be due to low refrigerant gas, a faulty compressor, dirty condenser coils, or a malfunctioning thermostat. Our technicians can diagnose the exact cause and recommend the most cost-effective solution.

How often should I service my refrigerator?

We recommend professional maintenance every 6-12 months. Regular servicing includes cleaning condenser coils, checking gas levels, inspecting door seals, and testing thermostat accuracy. This extends your fridge's lifespan and prevents costly breakdowns.

Is fridge gas recharge safe?

Yes, when performed by certified technicians. Modern refrigerators use R600a or R134a refrigerant gases, which are safe when handled correctly. Our technicians are trained in proper gas handling procedures and safety protocols.

My fridge is making loud noises - is it serious?

Loud noises from your fridge can indicate compressor issues, fan motor problems, or loose components. While not always an emergency, persistent loud noises should be checked promptly to prevent further damage and expensive repairs.
